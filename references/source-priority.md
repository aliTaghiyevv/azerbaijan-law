# Source Priority

Use official Azerbaijan government and court sources before secondary sources.

## Core Rule

Prefer current official Azerbaijani-language sources. Use English or Russian pages only when they are official and suitable for orientation; if exact wording matters, verify against the official Azerbaijani text.

When sources conflict, use `legal-hierarchy.md`: higher authority beats lower authority, later amendments beat earlier text, and specific rules usually beat general rules.

## Primary Source Map

### Consolidated Legal Acts

- `e-qanun.az`: Primary starting point for consolidated texts of the Constitution, codes, laws, presidential decrees, Cabinet decisions, and many normative acts.
- Use it to verify current article text, amendment history, act number, adoption date, and article-level citations.
- Treat consolidated text as the main working source, but check amendment instruments or issuing authority pages when effective dates, transitional provisions, or repeal status matter.

### State Publication and Executive Acts

- `president.az`: Presidential decrees, orders, official announcements, and presidential legal acts. Use when the question involves presidential authority, appointments, state programs, decrees, or orders.
- `nk.gov.az`: Cabinet of Ministers decisions, resolutions, rules, procedures, and official Cabinet materials. Use for implementation rules, administrative procedures, tariffs, lists, and state program implementation.
- `justice.gov.az`: Ministry of Justice materials, registration of normative legal acts, legal policy materials, and justice-sector information.
- `meclis.gov.az`: Milli Majlis materials, draft-law context, parliamentary records, and law adoption context. Use for legislative background, not as a substitute for the final official legal text.

### Courts

- `constcourt.gov.az`: Constitutional Court decisions and interpretations. Use when constitutionality, binding interpretation, separation of powers, fundamental rights, or constitutional review is relevant.
- `supremecourt.gov.az`: Supreme Court decisions, plenary resolutions, summaries, and judicial interpretation materials. Use for court practice, cassation-level reasoning, and interpretive guidance.
- `e-mehkeme.gov.az`: E-court and case-related services where available. Use for procedural context and case lookup when relevant.
- Court materials are not a substitute for statutes or codes. Classify whether a court source is binding, persuasive, procedural, or merely informational.

### Corporate, Registry, and Business

- `taxes.gov.az`: State Tax Service materials, taxpayer registration, tax guidance, forms, e-services, VAT, simplified tax, profit tax, and tax administration materials.
- `e-taxes.gov.az`: Taxpayer e-services and registration workflows. Use for current filing or registration procedures, but cite legal authority from the Tax Code or official rules where possible.
- `asan.gov.az`: ASAN service descriptions, required documents, service fees, and practical administrative steps. Use for procedure orientation, then verify legal basis from acts or responsible agency sources.
- `e-gov.az`: E-government portal services and cross-agency digital procedures. Use for service availability and procedure routing.

### Banking, Finance, Insurance, and Securities

- `cbar.az`: Central Bank of Azerbaijan normative acts, prudential rules, payment systems, banking, non-bank credit organizations, insurance, capital markets, currency regulation, and financial-sector guidance.
- Use the Central Bank source for sector-specific obligations, licensing, reporting, prudential standards, and regulator practice.
- Verify statutory authority against relevant laws and codes when legal obligations, penalties, licensing status, or enforcement exposure matter.

### Employment, Social Protection, and Migration

- `sosial.gov.az`: Ministry of Labor and Social Protection materials, employment, labor protection, social insurance, pensions, and social benefits.
- `dma.gov.az`: State Migration Service materials, residence permits, work permits, migration registration, citizenship-related procedure information, and migration compliance.
- `migration.gov.az` may appear in older materials. Prefer the current official State Migration Service domain and verify current URLs.
- For employment matters, cite the Labor Code and implementing acts first, then agency guidance for procedure.

### Taxes, Customs, and Public Revenue

- `taxes.gov.az`: Tax Code guidance, tax administration, taxpayer obligations, forms, and explanatory materials.
- `customs.gov.az`: State Customs Committee materials, customs procedures, declarations, tariffs, import/export controls, and customs e-services.
- Use agency pages for current forms and administrative process, but cite the Tax Code, Customs Code, or normative acts for legal duties and penalties.

### Public Procurement and State Assets

- `etender.gov.az`: Public procurement notices, tenders, procurement documents, and procurement portal workflows.
- Use procurement portal materials for factual tender data. Cite procurement legislation and official rules for legal requirements, remedies, deadlines, and disqualification rules.

### Intellectual Property, Data, Communications, and Technology

- `copat.gov.az`: Intellectual Property Agency materials for patents, trademarks, copyright, and related registrations.
- `mincom.gov.az`: Ministry of Digital Development and Transport materials for communications, digital policy, transport-related regulation, and relevant licensing or state programs.
- `cert.az`: Cybersecurity and incident-related official materials where relevant.
- For data protection and privacy, verify the governing law and any regulator guidance from official state sources before relying on commentary.

### Real Estate, Notary, and Civil Status

- `emlak.gov.az` or the current official property/state service domain: real estate registration, cadastral information, and state property materials.
- `asan.gov.az`: Practical service steps for notary, civil status, real estate, and document services.
- For title, notarization, inheritance, marriage, or civil status matters, cite the Civil Code, Family Code, procedural rules, and official service requirements. Recommend lawyer or notary review for legally consequential transactions.

### Criminal, Administrative Offenses, and Enforcement

- Use `e-qanun.az` for the Criminal Code, Criminal Procedure Code, Code of Administrative Offenses, and enforcement-related laws.
- Use court sources for interpretation and practice.
- Use relevant ministry or agency sources for procedure only after identifying the controlling code or statute.
- Escalate to licensed counsel for criminal exposure, administrative penalties, enforcement actions, searches, seizures, detention, or appeal deadlines.

## Search Tactics

- Search by Azerbaijani legal terms first, then Russian or English if needed.
- Search by act number, article number, and exact Azerbaijani title when available.
- For amended provisions, search both the consolidated article and the amendment act.
- For procedures, search the responsible agency page and then trace back to the legal act cited by that agency.
- For court practice, search court site materials and identify the court level, decision date, case or decision number, and whether the decision has broader interpretive value.

## Secondary Sources

Use law-firm articles, blogs, commercial databases, commentary, AI summaries, and unofficial translations only for orientation.

Do not present a secondary-source statement as Azerbaijan law unless it has been verified against official text. If no official source can be found, say so and label the conclusion as unverified or tentative.

Secondary sources are useful for:

- Finding the relevant act or article.
- Understanding practical context.
- Identifying recent amendments or regulator practice to verify.
- Finding English terminology for bilingual explanations.

## Source Capture

Record these fields whenever possible:

- Official title of the act or decision.
- English title if using a translation, marked official or unofficial.
- Act number, decision number, or case identifier.
- Adoption date.
- Effective date.
- Latest amendment or version date.
- Article, chapter, clause, paragraph, or section.
- Issuing authority.
- Source type: normative legal act, court decision, regulator guidance, service description, commentary, or unofficial translation.
- URL and access date.

## Recency Rule

If the user's request concerns current legal rights, obligations, deadlines, penalties, registration, licensing, tax, employment, immigration, corporate, finance, public procurement, litigation, or regulatory compliance, verify current law before answering.

Use exact dates in the answer when the timing matters. If the latest amendment or effective date cannot be verified, state that limitation.

## Red Flags

Do additional verification or recommend licensed counsel when the task involves:

- Court filing, appeal, limitation period, or procedural deadline.
- Criminal or administrative offense exposure.
- Tax assessment, audit, penalty, or filing obligation.
- Immigration status, residence, work permit, deportation, or citizenship.
- Employment termination, mass layoff, workplace injury, or discrimination.
- Real estate title, mortgage, inheritance, or notarized transaction.
- Banking, insurance, securities, currency control, or other regulated financial activity.
- Public procurement challenge, bid disqualification, or blacklist risk.
- Sanctions, AML, anti-corruption, data breach, or cybersecurity incident.
