# Legal Hierarchy

Use this hierarchy to reason about authority and conflicts under Azerbaijan law.

## Authority Order

Generally prioritize:

1. Constitution of the Republic of Azerbaijan.
2. Constitutional laws.
3. International treaties binding Azerbaijan, within their applicable constitutional/legal status.
4. Codes and laws adopted by the Milli Majlis.
5. Presidential decrees and orders.
6. Cabinet of Ministers decisions and resolutions.
7. Ministry, committee, agency, municipality, and regulator normative acts within delegated authority.
8. Court decisions and interpretive materials, with Constitutional Court and Supreme Court materials treated according to their institutional role and binding effect.

## Conflict Handling

When sources conflict:

- Prefer higher authority over lower authority.
- Prefer later amendments over earlier text at the same authority level.
- Prefer specific rules over general rules when both apply.
- Check whether a lower act was issued under delegated authority.
- Check whether a court or constitutional interpretation changes how a rule applies.

## Norm Type Checks

Classify each source before relying on it:

- Normative legal act.
- Non-normative act.
- Act of normative nature.
- Court decision or interpretive position.
- Regulator guidance or administrative practice.
- Secondary commentary.

Do not treat commentary or unofficial translation as binding law.
