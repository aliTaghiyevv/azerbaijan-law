# Research Workflows

## Quick Legal Question

1. Restate the issue in one sentence if useful.
2. Give a direct answer.
3. Cite the controlling authority.
4. State key caveats and missing facts.
5. Link sources.

## Legal Research Memo

Use:

- Issue
- Short Answer
- Facts/Assumptions
- Authorities
- Analysis
- Caveats
- Sources

Keep the analysis tied to cited authority. Separate what the law says from practical or strategic considerations.

## Version or Amendment Comparison

Use:

- Current act and article.
- Prior act/version or amendment instrument.
- Effective date of change.
- Textual change.
- Practical effect.
- Open questions.

When possible, cite both the consolidated current text and the amendment act.

## Compliance Checklist

Use:

- Requirement.
- Who it applies to.
- Trigger.
- Deadline or frequency.
- Filing/registration authority.
- Documents/evidence.
- Penalty or risk.
- Source.

Do not invent deadlines, forms, fees, or penalties. Verify them from official sources.

## Drafting Support

For contracts, policies, pleadings, notices, letters, or internal memos:

1. Ask for or infer the audience and governing language.
2. Identify mandatory legal terms or procedural requirements.
3. Draft in the requested language.
4. Add bracketed placeholders for missing facts.
5. Include a lawyer-review note for high-stakes filings or enforceability questions.
